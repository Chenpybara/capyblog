Editing is where the magic happens. Raw footage becomes a story. Here is my complete workflow from start to finish.

## Phase 1: Organization (10 minutes)

Before opening any editing software, I organize my files:

1. Create a folder: `YYYY-MM-DD_ProjectName`
2. Subfolders: `footage/`, `audio/`, `music/`, `exports/`, `assets/`
3. Copy all SD card media to `footage/`
4. Rename clips if needed

This takes five minutes and saves hours of searching later.

## Phase 2: Rough Cut (30-60 minutes)

In DaVinci Resolve, my rough cut process:

- Watch all footage at 2x speed
- Mark in/out points for usable clips
- Drag selects to the timeline
- Ignore color, audio levels, and pacing

The goal is to have a timeline that tells the complete story in order. It will be ugly. That's fine.

## Phase 3: Tightening (45 minutes)

Now I refine:

- Cut dead air and ums/ahs
- Add jump cuts where needed
- Adjust pacing (faster for b-roll montages, slower for tutorials)
- Add b-roll over talking-head sections

**Tip**: Watch your timeline with audio off. If it's still engaging, your visual pacing works.

## Phase 4: Enhancements (30-60 minutes)

### Text Overlays

I use simple white text with a subtle drop shadow. Less is more — I only add text when it adds clarity.

### Transitions

I use three transitions and no more:

1. **Cut** — 90% of the time
2. **Fade to black** — Scene changes
3. **Cross dissolve** — Montage sequences

### Music

Always slightly quieter than you think. Background music should be felt, not heard.

## Phase 5: Color Grade (15 minutes)

My go-to LUT:

- Contrast +10%
- Saturation +5%
- Lift (shadows) → slightly blue
- Gamma (mids) → slightly warm
- Gain (highlights) → neutral

I apply this globally, then adjust individual clips that need it.

## Phase 6: Export

| Setting      | Value       |
| :----------- | :---------- |
| Format       | H.264       |
| Resolution   | 4K          |
| Bitrate      | 45 Mbps     |
| Audio        | AAC 320kbps |

This workflow takes me about 3 hours from import to export. With practice, it gets faster. The key is consistency — using the same process every time builds muscle memory.
