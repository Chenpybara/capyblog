After testing dozens of cameras and accessories over the past year, here are the five pieces of gear I actually use every day for content creation.

## 1. Sony ZV-E1 — The Content Creator's Powerhouse

This full-frame camera is purpose-built for creators. It handles low light beautifully, has incredible autofocus, and the built-in stabilization means you can leave the gimbal at home.

- Resolution: 4K 120fps
- Sensor: Full-frame 12.1MP
- IBIS: 5-axis
- Weight: 483g

## 2. Sigma 24-70mm f/2.8 DG DN II

Skip the kit lens. This is the only lens most creators need. Sharp at all focal lengths, fast aperture for subject separation, and versatile enough for talking-head shots and b-roll alike.

## 3. DJI Mic 2

Wireless audio that just works. The transmitter doubles as a recorder, so even if your camera audio cuts out, you have a backup. 32-bit float recording means no more clipped audio.

## 4. Amaran 200d LED Light

For talking-head videos, lighting is everything. The Amaran 200d is bright enough to compete with window light, has a Bowens mount for softboxes, and costs less than a nice dinner out.

## 5. Sandisk 2TB Portable SSD

Editing off an external SSD changes everything. No more waiting for files to load. I keep all my active projects on this drive with room to spare.

### What About Budget Options?

If you're starting out, here's a setup under $500:

- **Camera**: Your smartphone
- **Mic**: Rode VideoMicro ($50)
- **Light**: Neewer ring light ($30)
- **Storage**: 1TB portable HDD ($50)

You don't need expensive gear to make great content. You need consistency and practice.
